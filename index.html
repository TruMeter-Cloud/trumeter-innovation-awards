Purpose

The Innovation Awards encourage everyone at Trumeter to contribute ideas that improve our products, processes, customer experience, manufacturing, sustainability, and ways of working.

💡

Open to Everyone

Innovation is not limited to Engineering. Great ideas can come from any team, any site, and any role.

⚙️

Practical Improvements

Small changes can make a big difference. We welcome simple ideas as well as bold concepts.

🚀

Future-Focused

The programme helps Trumeter explore new technologies, smarter products, and better ways to serve customers.

How It Works

Every 1–2 months, a new innovation subject is announced. Employees submit ideas, a judging panel reviews them, and winners receive recognition and small prizes.

Theme Announced

A new subject is shared, such as AI, sustainability, customer experience, or manufacturing efficiency.

Ideas Submitted

Employees use the form below to explain the problem, solution, and expected benefit.

Ideas Reviewed

The Innovation Review Board scores submissions using clear criteria.

Winners Celebrated

Winning ideas are announced in a company update and receive small prizes.

Best Ideas Progress

High-value ideas may be developed further with support from the relevant department.

Judging Criteria

Ideas are judged fairly using a simple scoring framework. Hover over highlighted words for guidance.

Innovation — 30%

Is the idea creative, different, or a smart improvement to how we currently work?

Business Impact — 25%

Does the idea create measurable value, such as cost saving, revenue growth, efficiency, or quality improvement?

Practicality — 20%

Can the idea realistically be implemented with available time, budget, and resources?

Customer Benefit — 15%

Will the idea improve the experience, reliability, support, or value delivered to customers?

Strategic Alignment — 10%

Does the idea support Trumeter’s technology, product, manufacturing, or commercial direction?

What Makes a Good Submission?

A strong idea clearly explains the problem, the solution, and the benefit. It does not need to be perfect. A practical idea that can be tested quickly is often better than a complex idea that never starts.
